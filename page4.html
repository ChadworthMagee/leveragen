Overview of Taiwanese Biotech Expansion into the U.S.

The Taiwanese biotechnology sector has evolved from a regional player into a globally competitive force, marked by strategic expansion into the United States. This evolution represents the culmination of decades of government support, R&D investment, and strategic vision to integrate Taiwan's manufacturing strengths with U.S. market opportunities.

Government Support Framework

The Taiwanese government, through the Ministry of Economic Affairs (MOEA) and its Biotechnology and Pharmaceutical Industries Promotion Office (BPIPO), has been instrumental in fostering industry growth.

  • • [^390] 172 companies recognized as biotech firms by MOEA
  • • Tax credits and R&D subsidies
  • • Taiwan Biotech Takeoff Plan (2009)

Market Performance

The sector has achieved significant growth, with total revenues reaching approximately $23.3 billion in 2023 and ambitions to grow into a $32 billion economy.

  • • [^394] Strong foundation in precision medicine
  • • Immunotherapy and digital health expertise
  • • Global integration strategy

Strategic Drivers for U.S. Market Entry

"Made in USA" Advantage

[^427] The U.S. BioSecure Act has created opportunities for Taiwanese companies with U.S. operations to position themselves as "friend-shored" alternatives to Chinese manufacturers.

  • • Avoid potential tariffs and trade barriers
  • • Qualify for government contracts
  • • Enhanced brand reputation

Tariff Policy Navigation

[^422] U.S. tariff policies have prompted Taiwanese companies to establish physical presence, mitigating supply chain risks and ensuring cost-effective market access.

  • • Supply chain resilience
  • • Cost optimization
  • • Competitive positioning

Market Access

[^423] The U.S. offers access to the world's largest pharmaceutical market, sophisticated clinical trial infrastructure, and deep investment capital pools.

  • • FDA approval as global validation
  • • Large patient populations
  • • Investment capital access

Common U.S. Expansion Models

Acquisitions of U.S. Companies

[^422] Direct acquisitions provide immediate access to established manufacturing capabilities, regulatory expertise, and distribution networks.

Case Study: Bora Pharmaceuticals
  • • Acquired Upsher-Smith Laboratories (Minnesota)
  • • Purchased Emergent BioSolutions facility (Maryland)
  • • Acquired Pyros Pharmaceuticals (New Jersey)
  • • Total investment: NT$8.58 billion

Establishing Subsidiaries and Offices

[^428] Measured entry through subsidiaries allows companies to build presence gradually while maintaining operational control.

Key Locations:
  • • San Diego, CA (PapiVax, OBI Pharma, Tanvex)
  • • San Francisco Bay Area (HanchorBio, Elixiron)
  • • Burlington, MA (PharmaEssentia)
  • • Ontario, CA (CH Biotech)
